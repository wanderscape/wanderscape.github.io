Crystal Clear Water

The waters of Samal Island is exceptionally clear, you can literally see the corals underneath the sea.

About Samal Island

How to get there?

Book a flight to Davao City from Manila, Clark, Cebu, Bacolod, Iloilo, Zamboanga, Cagayan de Oro, Dumaguete, Tagbilaran, Tacloban, Caticlan, Puerto Princesa, and Siargao through Philippine Airlines, Air Asia Philippines or Cebu Pacific. From Davao City, boats and ferries are available at Sta. Ana Wharf and Km. 11 Wharf in Sasa.

Where to stay in Samal Island?

There are more than 30 commercial resorts scattered along 116-kilometer of shoreline of Samal. Pearl Farm Beach Resort is famous for high-end tourists, while Paradise Island Resorts is popular for most general tourists due to its affordability and accessibility.

Isla Reta

How to get there

From Manila and Cebu, there are daily flights directly to Davao City. From Davao airport, catch a bus going to Ecoland Bus Terminal passes by Cabaguio or Dacudao road. Tell the driver to drop you down at Magsaysay port or ride a taxi to get there. There is a direct boat going to Isla Reta which costs P70 and will take you 45 minutes to get to the island.

What to expect

Isla Reta 2 has an idyllic and relaxing atmosphere, ideal for those who want to forget about the worries of work and city life. It has a white sand beach, turquoise waters, and rock formations dotting it. Wade or swim in the clear warm waters, have a picnic, get tanned, and take plenty of pictures of the rugged yet beautiful beachscape.

Talicud Island

How to get there

From Davao City, take a taxi to Sta. Ana Wharf near Magsaysay Park. Take a boat to Talikud Island which costs P60. When you arrive at the island, you can walk to the nearest beach resort or hire a tricycle or habal-habal to take you further which will cost around P100. 

What to expect

Bring your snorkelling gear so you can take advantage of the clear-as-crystal waters and take a peek at the many wonders below. If you have more time, Take introductory diving lessons so you can explore the many amazing dive sites around the island like Angel’s Cove, Mansud Wall and the Coral Gardens.

Kaputian Beach

How to get there

Board a jeep bound for Sasa Wharf, which costs around P10 to P15. There are regular ferries going to and from Samal Island. The fare is around P10 and takes only a few minutes. After arriving at the ferry terminal on Samal Island, you can hire a motorcycle with driver to go to the beach directly, the ride may cost around P75.

What to expect

Despite the crowds, especially during weekends and holidays, the beach still has a laid back vibe. Enjoy swimming or just wade in the waters, get a tan, have a cold drink, or read a book. You can also pitch a tent and camp with friends or family during your stay.
