Coron, Palawan

Dicantuman Beach

Mesmerizing view

The beach has fine white sand and coconut trees that will totally spice up your Instagram photos. It is not crowded with a lot of people since the beach is a part of the island hopping tour.

Crystal clear water

The water is really clear that you'd be able to look at the sand underneath. The water is very clean as well, a single piece of plastic won't get in your sight.

Twin Lagoon

How to get there

If you have a look at the menu of island hopping excursions posted all around Coron town, you'll be able to pick an option that includes this stop for P1200-P1700.

What to expect

The lagoon water is a mix of salt water from sea and fresh water from mountain, which makes you feel hot and cold at different points.

Kayangan Lake

How to get there

You can get to the lake through picking an island hopping excursion that includes this stop. To get the access to the crystal clear waters of Kayangan, you have to climb a 347-step stairs for 10 minutes.

What to expect

There's a little wooden walkway and platform to stash your things if you go for a swim. Don't expect privacy or quiet as the lagoon where the boats unload passengers looks like a mall parking lot at noon.

Maquinit Hot Spring

How to get there

From the town center, hire a tricycle to the hot spring. Tricycle rental will cost you P300 for a round-trip transportation. You can ask the driver to wait for you and take you back to the town center.

What to expect

Maquinit Hot Spring is the only known saltwater hot spring in the Philippines. The water comes from a volcano located underground and its temperature is about 40 degress Celsius.

Calauit Safari Park

  What is Calauit Safari Park? 

Calauit Safari Park was established on August 21, 1976 by Presidential Proclamation 1578 issued by President Ferdinand Marcos. It covers almost 3,800 hectares.

  How to get there? 

From Manila, book a flight to Busuanga airport. If your hotel is located in Coron, you can take a van/shuttle to Coron town. The entrance fee costs P200 for local tourists and P400 for foreigners. The use of land rover for the tour around the park costs P1000.

  What shall I expect? 

The park currently has giraffes, zebras, fresh water crocodiles, bushbuck, bear cats, mokeys, elans, waterbucks, Calamian deers, and a variety of birds roaming around freely. The tourists are explored around through a shuttle. The tourists are allowed to feed the giraffes.
